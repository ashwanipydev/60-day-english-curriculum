## Why Modals Matter

Modals make you sound **professional, not rude**.

Without modals: "Send me the file." ← sounds like a command
With modals: "Could you please send me the file?" ← sounds professional

---

## The 7 Essential Modals

| Modal | Use | Office Example |
|-------|-----|---------------|
| **Can** | Ability / Informal request | "Can you send this?" |
| **Could** | Polite request | "Could you please check this?" |
| **Should** | Advice / Suggestion | "You should review the report." |
| **Must** | Strong necessity | "We must finish by Friday." |
| **Will** | Promise / Future | "I will send it today." |
| **Would** | Very polite request | "Would you like to join the call?" |
| **May** | Permission (formal) | "May I share my screen?" |

## Upgrade Your Sentences

| Basic (Rude) | Professional (Modal) |
|-------------|---------------------|
| "Send me the file." | "Could you please send me the file?" |
| "Come to the meeting." | "Would you be able to join the meeting?" |
| "Finish this today." | "Can you please finish this today?" |
| "Give me an update." | "Could you share an update when possible?" |
| "Check this." | "Would you mind reviewing this?" |

---

## Important Grammar Rules

1. Modals do NOT take 's' for he/she/it:
   - ❌ "She cans do it."
   - ✅ "She **can** do it."

2. Modals are always followed by the base form (V1):
   - ❌ "You should to go."
   - ✅ "You should **go**."

3. For negative: Modal + **not**
   - "You shouldn't skip this."
   - "We can't attend."

## Practice

Convert these rude/basic sentences into polite professional ones:

1. "Do this now." → ________________
2. "Tell me the status." → ________________
3. "Come to my office." → ________________
4. "Don't do this." → ________________
5. "I want to leave early." → ________________

<details>
<summary>Suggested Answers</summary>

1. "Could you please do this now?"
2. "Would you be able to share the status?"
3. "Could you please come to my office?"
4. "I think we shouldn't do this."
5. "Would it be okay if I left a bit early today?"

</details>

## Key Takeaway

> Modals = politeness. In professional settings, always use modals for requests. **Could** and **Would** are your best friends in office English.
