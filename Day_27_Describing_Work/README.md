## How to Talk About What You Do

Being able to describe your work clearly is essential — in interviews, meetings, and networking.

---

## The STAR Format for Work Stories

| Letter | Stands For | Question It Answers |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|
| **S** | Situation | What was happening? |
| **T** | Task | What was your job? |
| **A** | Action | What did you do? |
| **R** | Result | What happened because of it? |

**Example:**
> **S:** "Our team was struggling to meet the deadline for the client project."
> **T:** "My task was to identify the bottleneck and fix it."
> **A:** "I reorganized the task list, held a quick sync with the team, and reallocated two tasks."
> **R:** "We submitted the project on time and the client was satisfied."

---

## Phrases for Describing Your Work

**What you do:**
- "I'm responsible for..."
- "My role involves..."
- "I handle..."
- "I work on..."

**What's in progress:**
- "Currently, I'm working on..."
- "We're in the process of..."
- "We're at the stage where..."

**What you achieved:**
- "We successfully completed..."
- "I managed to..."
- "As a result of my work, we..."

---

## Practice Task

Describe a recent work project using the STAR format. Speak for 2 minutes. Record yourself.

Topics if you're stuck:
- A challenge you solved this week
- A project you completed recently
- A meeting that went well or badly (and what you learned)
