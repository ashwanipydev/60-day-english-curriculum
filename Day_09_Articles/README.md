# Day 9 — Articles: A, An, The (The Tiny Words That Matter)

## Why Articles Are Hard for Hindi Speakers

**Hindi has NO articles.**

In Hindi, you say: "मैंने email भेजी" (literally: "I email sent")
In English, you must say: "I sent **an** email" or "I sent **the** email"

This is the #1 grammar area where Hindi speakers make constant mistakes.

---

## The 3 Rules

### Rule 1: A / An — First mention (indefinite)

Use when talking about something for the **first time** or in general.

- **A** = before consonant sounds: "**a** meeting", "**a** report", "**a** user"
- **An** = before vowel sounds: "**an** email", "**an** office", "**an** hour", "**an** update"

> Note: It's about the **SOUND**, not the letter. "An hour" (H is silent = vowel sound), "A university" (sounds like "you" = consonant sound).

### Rule 2: The — Specific / Both know what you mean

Use when both speaker and listener know the specific thing you're talking about.

- "Please check **the** email I sent." *(we both know which email)*
- "**The** meeting is at 3 PM." *(we both know which meeting)*
- "I spoke to **the** manager." *(specific manager, not any manager)*

### Rule 3: No Article — General ideas, names, most proper nouns

- "I drink **coffee**." *(coffee in general, not a specific cup)*
- "She works in **Delhi**." *(cities don't need 'the')*
- "**Information** is important." *(uncountable noun)*

---

## Memory Trick

> First mention → **A/An** ("I saw **a** dog")
> Second mention → **The** ("**The** dog was brown")

---

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|-------|-------|-----|
| "I sent mail to you." | "I sent **an** email to you." | First mention, vowel sound |
| "Please check the mail." | "Please check **the** email." | Specific email |
| "I need information." | "I need **information**." | Uncountable — correct! No article |
| "He is a senior." | "He is **senior** to me." | When used as adjective, no article |
| "I am having a doubt." | "I have **a** question." | 'doubt' = suspicion in English |

---

## A vs An — Quick Reference

| A | An |
|---|----|
| a meeting | an email |
| a report | an update |
| a client | an error |
| a project | an issue |
| a user | an hour |
| a team | an agreement |

## Practice

Fill in A, An, The, or nothing (—):

1. I attended _____ meeting this morning.
2. _____ meeting was very productive.
3. She sent _____ email to the client.
4. Please check _____ email she sent.
5. We need _____ information about this.
6. He works in _____ Hyderabad.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. a
2. The
3. an
4. the
5. — (no article, information is uncountable)
6. — (no article, city name)

</details>

---

## Speaking Task

Describe your **office or home** using articles correctly. Aim for 10 sentences. Record yourself and listen for missing or incorrect articles.

---

## Key Takeaway

> **A/An** = first time or general. **The** = specific, both know it. **No article** = general uncountable nouns and proper nouns. Practice this daily — it takes time to become automatic.
