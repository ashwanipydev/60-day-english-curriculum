## 3 Types of Verbs You Must Know

| Type | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **Be verbs** | am / is / are / was / were | I am a developer |
| **Action verbs** | eat, write, speak, work | She writes emails |
| **Helper verbs** | can, will, should, must | You should speak clearly |

---

## Hindi Comparison: Be Verbs

| Hindi | English |
|-------|---------|
| मैं हूँ | I **am** |
| वो है | He / She **is** |
| हम हैं | We **are** |
| तुम हो | You **are** |
| वो थे | They **were** |

---

## The #1 Hindi Speaker Mistake

Hindi speakers often **drop "am/is/are"** because Hindi verbs are different.

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "I developer" | "I **am** a developer" |
| "She very smart" | "She **is** very smart" |
| "We in office" | "We **are** in the office" |

**Rule:** Every English sentence needs a verb. You cannot skip it.

## Action Verb Practice

Learn these 10 essential office action verbs:

| Verb | Hindi | Example |
|------|-------|---------|
| send | भेजना | I send emails every morning. |
| attend | उपस्थित होना | She attends all meetings. |
| write | लिखना | He writes reports. |
| review | समीक्षा करना | We review the document. |
| complete | पूरा करना | I complete tasks before the deadline. |
| schedule | समय निर्धारित करना | She schedules calls. |
| handle | संभालना | I handle client queries. |
| update | अपडेट करना | He updates the team daily. |
| present | प्रस्तुत करना | We present results on Friday. |
| approve | मंजूरी देना | The manager approves leaves. |

## Practice

Fill in the correct be verb (am / is / are / was / were):

1. I _____ a software developer.
2. She _____ in a meeting right now.
3. They _____ late yesterday.
4. We _____ ready for the presentation.
5. He _____ the team leader.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. am
2. is
3. were
4. are
5. is

</details>

---

## Speaking Task

Introduce yourself **5 times**, improving each time:

> "My name is ___. I am a ___. I work at ___. I live in ___."

Each time, try to add one more sentence. By the 5th time, make it 5–6 sentences.

---

## Key Takeaway

> Never skip the verb. Every English sentence must have one — especially **am / is / are**.
