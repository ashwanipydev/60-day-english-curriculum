## The 10-Minute Challenge

By now, you can handle 2–3 minutes of English speaking. Today: **10 uninterrupted minutes**.

This is the clearest test of fluency — can you keep going?

---

## How to Keep a Conversation Going

**When you run out of things to say:**
- Ask yourself a follow-up question: "But why is that? What does that mean for me?"
- Go deeper on a topic: "Let me expand on that point..."
- Change the subject naturally: "On a related note..." / "Speaking of which..."
- Share an example: "That reminds me of a time when..."
- Return to something you said earlier: "Going back to what I mentioned earlier..."

---

## 10-Minute Conversation Topics (pick one)

1. **Your career journey** — from when you started to where you are now to where you're going
2. **A challenge you faced** — the full story, what you did, what you learned
3. **Your opinions on 3 topics** — work culture, technology, personal development
4. **A day in your life** — morning to night, with detail and color
5. **What you've learned in 60 days** — your English journey story

---

## Conversation Connectors for Long Talks

| Purpose | Phrase |
|---------|--------|
| Adding detail | "What's interesting is that..." |
| Giving example | "For instance..." |
| Returning to main point | "Anyway, coming back to my main point..." |
| Changing topic | "On a different note..." |
| Wrapping up a point | "So in summary on this point..." |
| Moving on | "Let me move on to..." |

---

## Speaking Task

Set a timer for 10 minutes. Pick one of the 5 topics above. Speak continuously. Do NOT stop the timer if you stumble — keep going. Record it. Congratulations if you complete 10 minutes.
