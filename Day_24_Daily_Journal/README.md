## Why Write a Journal in English?

Writing daily bridges the gap between knowing English and **using** English. It forces you to:
- Put words together in real sentences
- Think in English (not translate)
- Track your progress over 60 days
- Build vocabulary naturally

---

## How to Write Your Journal Entry

**Target:** 100 words per day (about 10 sentences)
**Time needed:** 10–15 minutes
**When:** Evenings before sleep

---

## Journal Prompt Templates

**Week 4 Prompts (pick one each day):**

1. "Today at work, I... The most interesting thing was... I felt..."
2. "This week, I've been working on... It's going [well/badly] because..."
3. "A challenge I faced today was... I handled it by..."
4. "Today I learned something new: [what]. It matters because..."
5. "My energy today was [high/low] because... Tomorrow I plan to..."
6. "A conversation I had today: [who], [topic], [how it went]..."
7. "Something I'm proud of this week: ..."

---

## Sample Journal Entry

> "Today was a busy day at work. I had three meetings in the morning, which was a bit exhausting. The most important one was the project review with my manager. She was happy with my progress, but she suggested I should focus more on the documentation. In the afternoon, I completed the first draft of the report. I'm not 100% happy with it, but I'll revise it tomorrow. In the evening, I practiced my English for 30 minutes. I feel like my writing is improving slowly but steadily."

*Word count: 97 words — exactly right!*
