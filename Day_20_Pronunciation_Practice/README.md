## The Top 5 Pronunciation Errors Hindi Speakers Make

### Error 1: Adding Vowels to Consonant Clusters

Hindi words rarely start with two consonants together. So Hindi speakers add a vowel sound:

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "es-cool" | "school" |
| "is-peak" | "speak" |
| "es-train" | "strain" |
| "a-fraid" | "afraid" |

**Practice:** Hold your breath slightly before the cluster. "School" — no vowel before the 'S'.

---

### Error 2: Silent Letters

These letters are written but NOT spoken:

| Word | Silent Letter | Pronunciation |
|------|--------------|---------------|
| know | K | "no" |
| write | W | "rite" |
| hour | H | "our" |
| honest | H | "onest" |
| knife | K | "nife" |
| wrong | W | "rong" |
| thumb | B | "thum" |

---

### Error 3: B vs V Confusion

- ❌ "wery" → ✅ "very"
- ❌ "bideo" → ✅ "video"
- ❌ "wimple" → ✅ "simple" *(W not in this word, just checking V)*

---

### Error 4: Making All Syllables Equal

English is **stress-timed** — some syllables are long and strong, others are short and weak.

> "I WANT to go to the STORE" — WANT and STORE get emphasis, the rest are shorter.

Practise: "I'm GOING to the MARket" — NOT "I'm-go-ing-to-the-mar-ket" (equal length = sounds unnatural).

---

### Error 5: Ending Sounds Dropped

Hindi speakers often drop the final consonant:
- "work" sounds like "wor"
- "last" sounds like "las"
- "fact" sounds like "fac"

**Practice:** Slightly exaggerate the final consonant until it becomes natural.

## 10-Minute Pronunciation Routine

1. Read today's word list aloud (2 min)
2. Record yourself reading 5 sentences (3 min)
3. Listen back and identify errors (2 min)
4. Shadow a 1-minute YouTube clip (3 min)
