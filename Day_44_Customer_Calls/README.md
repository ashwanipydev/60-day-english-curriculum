## Client and Customer Communication

Talking to clients/customers in English requires: clarity, professionalism, and patience.

---

## Greeting a Customer/Client

**In person:**
> "Good morning! How can I help you today?"
> "Welcome! What brings you in?"

**On a call:**
> "Good morning, [Company] — this is [Name]. How can I assist you?"

---

## Understanding Their Need

- "Could you tell me a bit more about what you're looking for?"
- "So, if I understand correctly, you need [X]?"
- "Just to confirm — you'd like [X], by [date]?"

---

## When There's a Problem

**Acknowledging:**
- "I completely understand your frustration."
- "I'm sorry you've had this experience."
- "Thank you for bringing this to our attention."

**Taking action:**
- "Let me look into this right away."
- "I'll escalate this and get back to you within [timeframe]."
- "Here's what I can do for you..."

---

## Inquiry Call Scripts

**You calling a vendor/service:**
> "Hi, I'm calling to inquire about [product/service]. Could you give me some information about [pricing/availability/process]?"

**Following up:**
> "Hi, I'm following up on a query I raised earlier about [X]. Could you confirm the status?"

---

## Today's Speaking Challenge

Call a **real shop, restaurant, or service** (or pretend to). Ask about:
- Their hours
- Menu / product availability
- Pricing
- How to book/order

Speak only in English. Record yourself if possible.
