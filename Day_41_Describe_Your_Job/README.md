## The Ability to Explain Your Work Simply

Whether it's a job interview, family function, or networking event — you need to explain your job to people with different backgrounds.

---

## The Two Levels of Explanation

### Level 1: For Non-Technical / Non-Industry People
> Explain as if talking to your neighbor or relative who has no idea what you do.

**Example (Software Engineer):**
> "I write instructions for computers — called code — that tell apps and websites how to work. Think of it like writing a very detailed recipe that a computer follows exactly."

**Example (Finance Analyst):**
> "I look at a company's money — how much comes in, how much goes out — and I make sure everything adds up and that decisions are good for the business's future."

### Level 2: For Industry Professionals
> Use proper terminology. Focus on what makes your work interesting or unique.

**Example (Software Engineer):**
> "I'm a backend developer specializing in microservices architecture. I work on the server-side logic and API design for our SaaS platform."

---

## Practice: The "Grandparent Test"

Explain your job as if to your grandparent (or someone very unfamiliar with your field). If they would understand it, you've done it well.

---

## Useful Structures

- "Basically, I..."
- "In simple terms, my job is to..."
- "What I do is..."
- "Think of it like..."
- "The result of my work is..."

---

## Speaking Task

Record yourself explaining your job **twice**:
1. Version for a non-technical family member (1 minute)
2. Version for a professional in your field (1 minute)

Compare the two. Notice how you adjust vocabulary and depth.
