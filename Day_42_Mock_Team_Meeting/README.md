## The Two Roles in Every Meeting

### Role 1: The Facilitator (Meeting Leader)

Responsibilities:
- Open the meeting
- Keep it on track
- Invite quieter people to speak
- Summarize decisions
- Close with action items

**Facilitator phrases:**
- "Let's get started. Today's agenda has three items."
- "Can we have a quick update from [team/person]?"
- "Let's keep this moving — we have 30 minutes."
- "Great, let's capture that as an action item."
- "Any objections before we move on?"
- "Let me summarize what we've decided..."

---

### Role 2: The Participant (Team Member)

Responsibilities:
- Come prepared
- Speak up at the right time
- Listen actively
- Confirm your action items

**Participant phrases:**
- "I'd like to add something here."
- "Can I ask a quick question before we move on?"
- "From my end, the update is..."
- "I can take ownership of [task]."
- "Could we schedule a follow-up on this?"

---

## Mock Meeting Structure (3 minutes)

Practice this format:

**Opening (30 sec):** "Good morning everyone. Let's get started. Today we're covering [topic]. We have 3 minutes, so let's keep it tight."

**Update (1 min):** "[Project] status: We've completed [X]. Currently working on [Y]. Blocker: [Z]. Need: [help/decision]."

**Discussion (1 min):** Take a question. Answer it. Make a decision.

**Close (30 sec):** "Great. Action items: [Name] will [task] by [date]. Next meeting: [when]. Thanks everyone!"

---

## Speaking Task

Lead a full **3-minute mock team meeting** by yourself — play all the roles. Record it. Check your timing.
