## The Secret to Reading English Fast

**Don't translate every word.**

Most Hindi speakers read English like this:
> "The" = uh... "meeting" = बैठक "was" = था "productive" = उत्पादक...

This is slow and exhausting. The goal is to **read for meaning**, not word-by-word translation.

---

## 3 Reading Techniques

### Technique 1: Chunking

Read in **phrases**, not individual words.

| Slow (Word by Word) | Fast (Chunking) |
|--------------------|--------------------|
| "The" / "marketing" / "team" / "will" / "present" | "The marketing team" / "will present" / "the quarterly report" / "on Friday." |

Your eyes should jump from chunk to chunk, not word to word.

### Technique 2: Context Clues

You don't need to know every word. **Guess from context.**

> "The project was **stalled** due to technical issues and we couldn't move forward."

Even if you don't know "stalled", the rest of the sentence tells you it means "stuck/stopped."

### Technique 3: The Skimming Strategy

Before reading fully, do this:
1. Read the **title and headings** first
2. Read the **first and last sentence** of each paragraph
3. Then read the full article
4. Look up only words that **repeat** or seem **important**

## After Reading: The Speak-Back Method

After you read a short article (5 minutes), close it and **summarize it out loud in 5 sentences** without looking.

This combines reading + speaking practice in one exercise.

---

## Practice

Read this paragraph and answer the questions below:

> "The company's quarterly earnings report showed a significant increase in revenue compared to last year. The management team attributed this growth to the successful launch of two new products and improved customer retention strategies. However, operating costs also rose by 12%, which slightly reduced the overall profit margin."

Questions:
1. What increased according to the report?
2. What caused the growth?
3. What was the negative aspect mentioned?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. Revenue / earnings increased.
2. Two new product launches and better customer retention.
3. Operating costs rose by 12%, reducing profit margin.

</details>

---

## Speaking Task

Read one short article from BBC Learning English today. Then set a timer for 2 minutes and summarize it out loud without looking at the text.

---

## Key Takeaway

> Reading is a skill — it improves with daily practice. Read a little every day, use chunking, and always summarize aloud after reading. Quantity + consistency beats marathon sessions.
