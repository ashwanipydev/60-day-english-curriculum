## Today's Speaking Challenge

> Give your opinion on a movie you watched

---

## Expressing Opinions Professionally

In English-speaking environments, you're expected to **share your views** — not just agree.

---

## Opinion Phrases (Formal to Informal)

**Strong opinion:**
- "I strongly believe that..."
- "I'm convinced that..."
- "In my view, ..."

**Moderate opinion:**
- "I think/feel that..."
- "It seems to me that..."
- "My impression is that..."

**Tentative opinion:**
- "I'm not entirely sure, but..."
- "I might be wrong, but..."
- "It could be that..."

---

## Agreeing with Someone's Opinion

- "That's exactly how I see it."
- "I couldn't agree more."
- "You've made a really good point."
- "That makes a lot of sense."

## Disagreeing Respectfully

- "I see where you're coming from, but..."
- "That's an interesting perspective. I look at it differently though."
- "I'm not sure I'd agree — I think..."
- "With respect, I'd argue that..."

---

## Today: Movie Opinion Practice

Pick any movie or TV show you've watched. Answer these questions in English:

1. **What is it about?** (plot summary — 2-3 sentences)
2. **What did you like most?** (use opinion phrases)
3. **What could have been better?** (disagree with something politely)
4. **Would you recommend it?** (give a strong opinion)
5. **What did you learn or take away from it?**

---

## Speaking Task

Record a **2-minute movie review** using the structure above. Pretend you're a film critic explaining it to someone who hasn't seen it.
