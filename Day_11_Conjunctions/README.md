## Why Conjunctions Matter

Without conjunctions, your English sounds choppy:
> "I finished the report. I sent it. The client replied. It was positive."

With conjunctions, it flows:
> "I finished the report and sent it, **and** the client replied **with** positive feedback."

---

## Basic Conjunctions — FANBOYS

**F**or | **A**nd | **N**or | **B**ut | **O**r | **Y**et | **S**o

| Conjunction | Use | Example |
|-------------|-----|---------|
| **And** | Addition | "I speak Hindi and English." |
| **But** | Contrast | "I tried but it didn't work." |
| **Or** | Choice | "Should we call or email?" |
| **So** | Result | "The deadline is today, so I'm hurrying." |
| **Because** | Reason | "I was late because of traffic." |
| **Although** | Contrast | "Although it was hard, I finished it." |
| **If** | Condition | "If you need help, let me know." |

---

## Professional Connectors (Office English)

These make your writing sound more polished:

| Connector | Hindi | Use |
|-----------|-------|-----|
| **However,** | लेकिन | Contrast — "However, we need more time." |
| **Therefore,** | इसलिए | Result — "Therefore, I recommend we postpone." |
| **Moreover,** | इसके अलावा | Addition — "Moreover, the cost is higher." |
| **In addition,** | इसके साथ | Addition — "In addition, the team needs training." |
| **On the other hand,** | दूसरी तरफ | Contrast — "On the other hand, there are benefits." |
| **As a result,** | फलस्वरूप | Result — "As a result, we missed the deadline." |

## Practice

Connect these short sentences using a conjunction:

1. "I finished the work. I sent it late." → ________________
2. "You can call me. You can email me." → ________________
3. "The project is delayed. We are working hard." → ________________
4. "She is good at writing. She struggles with speaking." → ________________

<details>
<summary>Suggested Answers</summary>

1. "I finished the work, but I sent it late."
2. "You can call me or email me."
3. "The project is delayed, but we are working hard." / "Although the project is delayed, we are working hard."
4. "She is good at writing, but she struggles with speaking." / "She is good at writing, although she struggles with speaking."

</details>

---

## Writing Task

Take these 5 separate sentences and combine them into 2–3 longer sentences:

> "I read the report. It was 20 pages. It was detailed. I had questions. I emailed the manager."

---

## Speaking Task

Connect your simple sentences today. In your 2-minute monologue, challenge yourself to use at least 5 different conjunctions.

---

## Key Takeaway

> Conjunctions make your English sound **fluent and connected**, not choppy. In professional writing, use **However / Therefore / Moreover** to elevate your tone.
