## Today's Speaking Challenge

---

## Why Reading Aloud Builds Fluency

Reading silently doesn't train your mouth. Reading aloud does.

Benefits:
- Trains pronunciation muscle memory
- Improves reading speed and comprehension
- Builds confidence for public speaking
- Helps you identify unknown words in real time

---

## How to Read Aloud Effectively

**Step 1: Preview**
Read the paragraph silently first. Identify difficult words.

**Step 2: Read Slowly**
Speed is not the goal. Clarity is.

**Step 3: Focus on:**
- Sentence stress (which words are important?)
- Pausing at commas and full stops
- Not rushing through lists or numbers

**Step 4: Record and Review**
Listen back. Notice where you stumbled or sounded unnatural.

---

## Practice Paragraphs

**Paragraph 1 (Formal):**
> "The project team has made significant progress over the past two weeks. Key milestones have been achieved, including the completion of the initial research phase and the finalization of the project scope. The next phase will focus on implementation, which is expected to begin next Monday. All team members are encouraged to review the updated timeline and confirm their availability."

**Paragraph 2 (Conversational):**
> "I've been working on improving my English every day for the past month and I can already see the difference. I feel more confident in meetings and I don't freeze up as much when someone asks me a question. The key thing I've learned is that practice matters more than perfection. Just speak — even if you make mistakes — and you'll improve faster than you think."

---

## Speaking Task

Read **both paragraphs** aloud today. Record them. Listen back. Which sounds more natural to you? Where did you stumble? Practice those parts.
