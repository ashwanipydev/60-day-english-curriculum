## What is Shadowing?

**Shadowing** = speaking at the same time as a native speaker, trying to match their sound exactly.

It's used by diplomats, interpreters, and professional language learners. It's the fastest way to improve:
- Pronunciation
- Rhythm and speed
- Intonation (how your voice goes up and down)
- Natural phrasing

---

## How to Shadow Effectively

**Step 1: Choose a 1-minute clip**
- BBC Learning English news video
- TED Talk section
- YouTube short

**Step 2: Listen once without speaking**
Notice the rhythm. Where does the speaker pause? Where do they speed up?

**Step 3: Listen and speak simultaneously**
Don't worry if you miss words. Keep going. Match the rhythm.

**Step 4: Repeat 3–5 times with the same clip**
Each time, you'll catch more words and sound more natural.

**Step 5: Record yourself shadowing**
Compare your recording to the original.

## What to Focus On While Shadowing

1. **Stress** — Which words are strong? (usually nouns, verbs, adjectives)
2. **Rhythm** — English is not even — it has a beat
3. **Connections** — How words blend together ("gonna", "wanna", "didya")
4. **Pauses** — Where the speaker breathes, you breathe
