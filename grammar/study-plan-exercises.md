# ✍️ Study Plan & Exercises for Spelling, Reading, and Writing

Improving your English communication goes beyond just learning grammar rules. It requires consistent practice in reading, writing, and spelling. Below is a comprehensive guide with exercises and free resources to help you build these skills daily.

---

## 📝 Study Plan for Spelling & Writing

If you struggle with spelling or sentence structure, add these 10-minute daily habits:

### 1. Copywork (5 mins)
Take one paragraph from an English news article or a book and copy it by hand into a notebook. This physical act builds muscle memory for correct spelling and sentence structure. 
- *Why it works:* It forces you to slow down and observe how native speakers construct sentences.

### 2. The "Read Aloud" Check (5 mins)
After writing an email, a text message, or a journal entry, read it out loud to yourself. If you stumble or run out of breath, your sentence is likely too long or grammatically awkward.
- *Why it works:* Your ears are often better at catching mistakes than your eyes.

### 3. Common Misspellings List
Keep a running list of words you frequently misspell (e.g., *receive, separate, definitely, accommodate*) on your phone or notepad. Review it weekly.

---

## 📖 Exercises for Reading Comprehension

Reading is the best way to naturally absorb grammar and vocabulary.

### 1. Active Reading
Don't just read passively. While reading articles or stories, highlight new words in yellow and interesting phrases in green. Look up the meaning of the yellow words and try to use the green phrases in your own conversations.

### 2. The 3-Sentence Summary
After reading a short article or chapter, close the tab or book and try to write a 3-sentence summary:
- **Who/What** is it about?
- **What** happened or what was the main point?
- **Why** does it matter?

### 3. Read What You Love
Read about your hobbies (tech, sports, cooking, fashion) in English instead of your native language. You will learn faster when the topic naturally interests you, and it won't feel like "studying".

## 🆓 Free Resources to Practice

Here are some excellent, free online resources to help you practice writing, reading, and spelling:

### Reading & Vocabulary
- **[BBC Learning English](https://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/):** Great for short articles and news written specifically for learners.
- **[Voice of America (VOA) Learning English](https://learningenglish.voanews.com/):** News articles written with simplified vocabulary and audio versions.
- **[ReadTheory](https://readtheory.org/):** Free reading comprehension practice that adapts to your level.

### Writing & Spelling
- **[Grammarly](https://www.grammarly.com/):** Use the free version as a tool. When it corrects a mistake, *pay attention* to why it corrected it.
- **[Hemingway Editor](https://hemingwayapp.com/):** Great for checking if your sentences are too long or complex. Try to aim for a Grade 6-8 reading level for clear communication.
- **[Quizlet](https://quizlet.com/):** Create free flashcards for your "Common Misspellings List" to test yourself.

### Listening & Dictation (For Spelling)
- **[TED Talks](https://www.ted.com/talks):** Listen to a short segment, write down exactly what you hear, and then turn on the subtitles to check your spelling and accuracy (this is called dictation).
