# 🔤 The Ultimate Guide to English Spelling

English spelling can be tricky because it borrows from many different languages. However, learning a few basic rules can solve 80% of your spelling mistakes!

---

## 📜 5 Golden Rules of English Spelling

### 1. "I" before "E", except after "C"
This is the most famous English spelling rule.
- **Normal rule (ie):** believe, piece, chief, achieve.
- **After "C" (ei):** receive, ceiling, deceit, receipt.
- *Exceptions (words sounding like "A"):* neighbor, weigh, vein.

### 2. Dropping the silent "E"
When adding a suffix that starts with a vowel (like *-ing*, *-ed*, *-able*), drop the silent "E" at the end of the root word.
- hope + ing = **hoping**
- make + ing = **making**
- use + able = **usable**
- *Exception (if keeping the E preserves a soft C or G sound):* notice + able = **noticeable**, courage + ous = **courageous**.

### 3. Changing "Y" to "I"
If a word ends in a consonant + "Y", change the "Y" to an "I" before adding a suffix (unless the suffix starts with an "I", like *-ing*).
- happy + ness = **happiness**
- beauty + ful = **beautiful**
- try + es = **tries**
- *But with -ing:* try + ing = **trying** (keep the Y).

### 4. Doubling the final consonant
If a short word (one syllable) ends in a single vowel followed by a single consonant, double the consonant before adding a vowel suffix (*-ed*, *-ing*).
- stop + ed = **stopped**
- run + ing = **running**
- plan + ed = **planned**
- *Do not double if it ends in W, X, or Y:* snow -> snowing, fix -> fixed.

### 5. Plurals (S vs. ES)
- **Normal:** Add **-s** (cats, books, cars).
- **Ends in ch, sh, s, x, or z:** Add **-es** (matches, dishes, buses, boxes).
- **Ends in consonant + O:** Usually add **-es** (potatoes, heroes, tomatoes).

## ✍️ Spelling Exercises & Study Plan

If spelling is your main weakness, spend 15 minutes a day on these exercises:

### Exercise 1: The "Look, Say, Cover, Write, Check" Method (5 mins)
1. **Look** at the correct spelling of a difficult word (e.g., *accommodate*).
2. **Say** it out loud.
3. **Cover** the word with your hand.
4. **Write** it down from memory.
5. **Check** if you were right. If wrong, repeat the process.

### Exercise 2: Dictation Practice (10 mins)
1. Go to YouTube and find a short video (like a TED-Ed animation or BBC News clip).
2. Play 10 seconds of the video, pause, and **write down exactly what you heard**.
3. Turn on the closed captions (CC) and compare your writing to the subtitles.
4. Highlight the words you misspelled and add them to your "Common Misspellings" list.

### Exercise 3: Root Word Expansion
Take a root word and practice adding different suffixes to test the spelling rules.
- *Example:* **Happy** -> happiness, happily, happier, happiest.
- *Example:* **Run** -> running, runner, outrun.
