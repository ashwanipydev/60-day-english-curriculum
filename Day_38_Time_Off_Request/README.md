## Making Work Requests the Professional Way

How you ask matters as much as what you ask.

---

## The Structure of a Good Request

1. **Context** — Why you need it
2. **The request** — Clearly what you're asking
3. **Impact consideration** — Show you've thought about work impact
4. **Offer** — How you'll manage the gap

---

## Asking for Time Off

**Script:**
> "Hi [Manager], I wanted to ask about taking a day off on [date]. My [reason: family visit/appointment/etc.]. I've checked my schedule and there are no critical deliverables due on that day. I'll make sure to complete [task] before I leave and [colleague] can cover [if needed]. Would that be okay?"

---

## Other Work Requests

**Asking for more time on a deadline:**
> "I wanted to flag that the [task] might need a little more time than expected. I've hit [specific challenge]. Would it be possible to extend the deadline to [new date]? I'm committed to delivering high quality and want to make sure it's right."

**Asking for resources:**
> "I've been working on [project] and realized we might need [resource/tool/help]. It would allow us to [benefit]. Would that be something we could explore?"

**Asking for feedback:**
> "I'd really value your perspective on my work. Would you have 15 minutes sometime this week for a quick feedback session?"

---

## Saying No Professionally

Sometimes you need to push back. Here's how:
- "I appreciate you thinking of me for this, but I'm at capacity right now."
- "I'd love to help, but I have [commitments] this week. Could we revisit next week?"
- "I want to make sure I deliver quality work — could we prioritize between this and [other task]?"

---

## Speaking Task

**Roleplay:** You need to take next Friday off for a family event. Your team has a project review meeting that day. Practice the full conversation with your manager — asking, justifying, and proposing a solution.
