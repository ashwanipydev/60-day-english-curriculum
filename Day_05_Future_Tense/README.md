## 3 Ways to Talk About the Future

### 1. Will — Decision right now / Promise

- **Formula:** Subject + **will** + V1
- **Use:** Spontaneous decision, promise, prediction

| Example | Hindi |
|---------|-------|
| I will call you. | मैं तुम्हें call करूँगा। |
| I will send the report by 5 PM. | मैं 5 बजे तक report भेजूँगा। |
| It will be a good meeting. | यह एक अच्छी meeting होगी। |

### 2. Going to — Plan already decided

- **Formula:** Subject + **am/is/are going to** + V1
- **Use:** Pre-planned action, something you already decided

| Example | Hindi |
|---------|-------|
| I am going to attend the conference. | मैं conference में जाने वाला हूँ। |
| She is going to resign. | वो इस्तीफा देने वाली है। |
| We are going to launch next month. | हम अगले महीने launch करने वाले हैं। |

### 3. Present Continuous for Future — Fixed appointment

- **Formula:** Subject + **am/is/are** + V**-ing** *(with a time word)*
- **Use:** Appointment or schedule already fixed

| Example | Hindi |
|---------|-------|
| I am meeting the client on Friday. | मेरी Friday को client के साथ meeting है। |
| She is travelling to Mumbai tomorrow. | वो कल Mumbai जा रही है। |

---

## When to Use Which?

| Situation | Form | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Just decided right now | **will** | "I will help you with that." |
| Already planned before | **going to** | "I'm going to submit it tonight." |
| Fixed in calendar | **Present Continuous** | "I'm presenting at 10 AM." |

---

## Hindi Map

- "मैं करूँगा" = I will do / I am going to do
- Context decides which English form to use — but both are correct most of the time.

---

## Practice

Choose the best future form:

1. Your boss just asked you to help. You say: "I _____ (help) you right away."
2. You planned last week to attend a seminar. "I _____ (attend) the seminar on Thursday."
3. You have a calendar invite. "I _____ (meet) HR at 11 AM."

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. will help
2. am going to attend
3. am meeting

</details>

---

## Speaking Task

Plan your **tomorrow** out loud. Speak **10 sentences** using all 3 future forms — at least 3 sentences with each form.

---

## Key Takeaway

> **Will** = just decided. **Going to** = already planned. **Present Continuous** = fixed appointment. All three are correct — context decides which sounds most natural.
