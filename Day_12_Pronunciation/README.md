## English Sounds Hindi Doesn't Have

These are the 4 pronunciation challenges specific to Hindi speakers:

---

### 1. The TH Sound

Hindi has no TH sound — so speakers replace it with D or T.

| Type | How to Make It | Examples |
|------|---------------|---------|
| Unvoiced TH | Tongue between teeth, blow air | **th**ink, **th**ree, **th**anks |
| Voiced TH | Tongue between teeth, vibrate throat | **th**is, **th**at, **th**ere |

**Common errors:**
- ❌ "dink" → ✅ "think"
- ❌ "dat" → ✅ "that"
- ❌ "tree" → ✅ "three"

**Practice:** Say slowly: "The thirty-three thieves thought they thrilled the throne."

---

### 2. V vs W

Hindi has no W sound like in English.

| Sound | How to Make It | Examples |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **V** | Teeth on lower lip | **v**ery, **v**ideo, **v**oice |
| **W** | Lips rounded, push out | **w**ork, **w**eek, **w**ater |

**Common errors:**
- ❌ "wery good" → ✅ "very good"
- ❌ "wideo call" → ✅ "video call"

**Practice:** "Victor's violin vivifies vibrant vocal varieties."

---

### 3. Short vs Long Vowels

| Short | Long |
|-------|------|
| **i**t (छोटा i) | **ea**t (लंबा ee) |
| s**i**t | s**ea**t |
| sh**i**p | sh**ee**p |
| b**i**t | b**ea**t |

Hindi speakers often make all vowels long. Practice the short, clipped sounds.

---

### 4. Word Stress

Stress the **wrong** syllable = people misunderstand you.

| Word | As Noun | As Verb |
|------|---------|---------|
| present | **PRE**-sent | pre-**SENT** |
| record | **RE**-cord | re-**CORD** |
| project | **PRO**-ject | pro-**JECT** |
| protest | **PRO**-test | pro-**TEST** |
| permit | **PER**-mit | per-**MIT** |

**English is stress-timed:** stressed syllables are longer; unstressed ones are shorter. Hindi is syllable-timed (all equal). This is the biggest rhythm difference.

---

## Daily Pronunciation Drill (15 min)

1. Read tongue twisters aloud — slowly, then faster
2. Record yourself on your phone
3. Compare with a native speaker saying the same thing (YouTube)
4. Repeat the same clip 3 times

---

## Top Tongue Twisters for Hindi Speakers

1. "The thirty-three thieves thought they thrilled the throne." *(TH practice)*
2. "Whether the weather is warm, whether the weather is hot." *(W + TH)*
3. "Victor's violin vivifies vibrant vocal varieties." *(V vs W)*
4. "She sells seashells by the seashore." *(S + SH sounds)*

## Speaking Task

Record yourself reading 5 sentences from Day 1–11. Then:
1. Listen back
2. Identify 3 sounds you pronounced wrongly
3. Practice those 3 sounds 20 times each

---

## Key Takeaway

> You don't need a perfect accent. You need to be **understood**. Focus on TH sounds, V/W, word stress, and you'll be clearly understood by any English speaker.
