## You Don't Need a Partner to Practice Speaking

Most people wait for a language exchange partner or class. Don't wait — practice solo.

---

## Method 1: The Two-Chair Technique

Set up two chairs. Sit in Chair A and play yourself. Get up, sit in Chair B and play the other person.

Have a full conversation: ask → answer → respond. Move between chairs for each turn.

**Example conversations to practice:**
- Job interview
- Asking manager for feedback
- Explaining a delay to a client
- Introducing yourself to a new colleague

---

## Method 2: WhatsApp Voice Notes to Yourself

Create a group chat with just yourself. Send voice notes as if talking to someone. Listen back. This simulates real conversation.

---

## Method 3: Mirror Conversation

Stand in front of a mirror. Pretend the person in the mirror is your colleague/manager/client. Ask them questions. Answer as them. Keep the conversation going for 3 minutes.

---

## Practice Scripts

### Script A: Meeting a New Colleague
> You: "Hi, I'm [Name]. I just joined the [team]. Nice to meet you!"
> Them: "Welcome! I'm [Name]. How are you finding it so far?"
> You: "It's great! Everyone is very helpful. What team are you in?"
> Them: "I'm in [team]. How about you — what will you be working on?"
> You: "I'll be handling [task/project]. Looking forward to working together."

### Script B: Asking for Clarification in a Meeting
> You: "Before we move on, could I ask for a quick clarification?"
> Manager: "Of course, go ahead."
> You: "When we said the deadline is Friday, did we mean end of business Friday, or by Friday morning?"
> Manager: "Good question — by end of business on Friday."
> You: "Perfect, thank you. I just wanted to make sure."

---

## Speaking Task

Choose one script above. Practice it 5 times. Record the last attempt. Notice how much smoother it sounds.
