## The Job Interview in English

Whether or not you're job hunting, interview practice is the best fluency exercise — it demands clear, organized, confident speaking.

---

## Common Interview Questions + Strong Answers

### Q1: "Tell me about yourself."
**Structure:** Present → Past → Future
> "I'm currently working as a [role] at [company], where I [key responsibilities]. Before this, I [previous experience]. I'm excited about [what you're looking for next]."

### Q2: "What's your greatest strength?"
> "My strongest skill is [X]. For example, in my last project, I [specific example showing the skill], and the result was [outcome]."

### Q3: "What's a weakness you're working on?"
> "One area I've been focused on improving is [honest weakness]. I've been [what you're doing about it], and I've already seen [progress/result]."

### Q4: "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
*(Use your Day 53 career plan answer!)*

### Q5: "Why do you want this role?"
> "I'm drawn to this role because [genuine reason]. My background in [area] aligns well with [what the role needs]. I'm particularly excited about [specific aspect]."

### Q6: "Do you have any questions for us?"
Always say yes! Ask:
- "What does success look like in this role in the first 90 days?"
- "What's the team culture like?"
- "What are the opportunities for growth here?"

---

## Speaking Task

Do a **full mock job interview** — play both roles (interviewer and candidate). Cover all 6 questions above. Record the whole thing. Aim for 8–10 minutes total.
