## Today's Speaking Challenge

---

## What Makes a TED Talk Powerful?

TED Talks are famous for being clear, compelling, and memorable. The techniques aren't secret — you can learn them.

---

## The 4 TED Elements

### 1. A Clear Central Idea

One sentence that captures everything:
> "My talk is about why small habits lead to big changes."
> "I want to show you why English fluency starts with confidence, not grammar."

### 2. A Story or Personal Hook

Start with something real:
> "Three years ago, I couldn't hold a conversation in English. Today, I lead international meetings. Here's what changed."

### 3. Simple, Powerful Language

Avoid jargon. Use short sentences. Use pauses for effect.

> "English is not difficult." *(pause)* "It's unfamiliar. And unfamiliarity is fixed by one thing." *(pause)* "Repetition."

### 4. A Strong Close

End with a call to action or a memorable line:
> "So my challenge to you is this: speak one sentence in English every day. Not ten. Not a hundred. One. Start there."

---

## Sample Topic Ideas for Your Talk

- "Why learning English changed my professional life"
- "One habit that made me better at my job"
- "What my commute taught me about productivity"
- "Why asking questions is more important than having answers"
- "The power of saying 'I don't know' at work"

---

## Speaking Task

Pick one topic. Prepare a **2-minute talk** following the TED structure:
1. Central idea (1 sentence)
2. Story/hook (30 seconds)
3. Your 3 key points (1 minute)
4. Strong close (30 seconds)

Record it. Watch it back. Notice your energy and pace.
