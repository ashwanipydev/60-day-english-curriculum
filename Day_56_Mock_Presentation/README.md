## The Full Presentation: Putting It All Together

Today is a big challenge — a full **4-minute presentation** on any topic.

This combines everything you've learned:
- Clear structure (open, body, close)
- Confident delivery
- Visual language ("As you can see...")
- Handling transitions
- Strong close

---

## 4-Minute Presentation Structure

```
[0:00–0:30] Opening — Hook + what you'll cover
[0:30–1:00] Point 1 — Idea + explanation + example
[1:00–1:45] Point 2 — Idea + explanation + example
[1:45–2:30] Point 3 — Idea + explanation + example
[2:30–3:00] Summary — Recap key points
[3:00–3:30] Conclusion — Final message + call to action
[3:30–4:00] Q&A invitation + close
```

---

## Topic Suggestions

Choose something you know well:
- "How to be more productive at work"
- "The benefits of learning a new language"
- "My experience in [your industry] and what I've learned"
- "Why [habit/skill] is essential for [professionals]"
- "3 things I wish I knew when I started my career"

---

## Delivery Tips

- **Pace:** Slower than you think. Pause for effect.
- **Energy:** Higher than your normal conversation voice
- **Eye contact:** Look at the camera (or at a point on the wall)
- **Gestures:** Use your hands naturally
- **Pauses:** Strategic silence is powerful

---

## Speaking Task

Prepare and record a **4-minute presentation**. No scripts — use keywords/bullet points only. Record it. Watch it back. This is your most important speaking exercise before the final challenge.
