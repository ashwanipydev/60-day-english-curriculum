## Meeting Language: What to Say and When

Meetings are where professional English matters most. This is where most Hindi speakers stay quiet — and lose out.

---

## Joining a Meeting

| Situation | Phrase |
|-----------|--------|
| On time | "Good morning everyone. Ready to start?" |
| Joining late | "I apologize for being late. I was on another call." |
| Requesting recap | "Could someone quickly catch me up on what was discussed?" |

---

## During a Meeting

**Adding your opinion:**
- "If I may add something here..."
- "I'd like to share my perspective on this."
- "Building on what [Name] said..."
- "I have a slightly different view on this..."

**Asking for clarification:**
- "Could you clarify what you mean by [X]?"
- "Just to make sure I understand correctly..."
- "Are you saying that...?"

**Agreeing:**
- "That's a great point."
- "I completely agree."
- "Absolutely."

**Disagreeing politely:**
- "I see your point, but I think..."
- "I respectfully disagree because..."
- "There might be another way to look at this..."

---

## Handling Being Late to a Meeting

**Script for today's challenge:**

> "I'm so sorry for being late. I was finishing up an urgent call with a client that ran over. I apologize for the inconvenience. Could someone quickly fill me in on what I missed, or shall I just listen and catch up?"

---

## Ending a Meeting

- "Let's wrap up."
- "To summarize what we've decided today..."
- "The action items are: [list them]"
- "Who is responsible for [X]?"
- "Let's reconnect on this on [date]."

---

## Speaking Task

**Roleplay scenario:** You're 10 minutes late to an important project meeting.
- Explain why you're late (professionally)
- Ask to be caught up briefly
- Add one contribution to the discussion
- Confirm your action item

Practice this roleplay 3 times. Record the final version.
