## The Big Difference

| Language | Order | Example |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Hindi | Subject + Object + Verb | मैं खाना **खाता हूँ** |
| English | Subject + Verb + Object | I **eat** food |

## The Golden Formula

```
S  +  V   +  O
I  + eat  + food
She + reads + books
We + attend + meetings
```

---

## Lesson

**Hindi brain habit:** In Hindi, the verb comes at the END.
In English, the verb comes SECOND — right after the subject.

This one change fixes 80% of Hindi-speaker sentence errors.

**More examples:**

| Hindi | Wrong English | Correct English |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| मैं पानी पीता हूँ | I water drink | **I drink water** |
| वो report भेजती है | She report sends | **She sends the report** |
| हम meeting करते हैं | We meeting do | **We have a meeting** |

## Practice

Convert these Hindi sentences to English:

1. मैं पानी पीता हूँ → ________________
2. वो English बोलती है → ________________
3. हम office जाते हैं → ________________
4. मैं रोज काम करता हूँ → ________________
5. वो emails check करती है → ________________

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. I drink water
2. She speaks English
3. We go to office
4. I work every day
5. She checks emails

</details>

## Speaking Task (10 min)

Say **10 sentences** about your daily routine using S+V+O structure out loud.

Examples:
- "I wake up at 7 AM."
- "I drink tea in the morning."
- "I check my emails at 9."
- "I attend meetings in the afternoon."

**Record yourself** on your phone. Listen back. Did you put the verb before the object?

---

## Key Takeaway

> In English, **the verb always comes second** in a statement. Never let the object jump in front of the verb.
