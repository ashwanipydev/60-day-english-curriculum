## Today's Speaking Challenge

> Write 3 professional emails — follow-up, update, and meeting request

---

## The 3 Emails You Write Every Week

### Email 1: The Follow-Up

```
Subject: Follow-up: [Topic]

Hi [Name],

I hope you're doing well. I'm following up on my previous email 
regarding [topic].

Could you please share an update at your earliest convenience?

Thank you.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

---

### Email 2: The Status Update

```
Subject: Update on [Project/Task Name]

Hi [Name/Team],

Quick update on [project/task]:

Current status: [brief update]
Next steps: [what will happen next]
Expected completion: [date]

Please feel free to reach out if you have any questions.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

---

### Email 3: The Meeting Request

```
Subject: Meeting Request – [Topic]

Hi [Name],

I hope this finds you well.

I'd like to schedule a brief meeting to discuss [topic]. 
Could you please let me know your availability this week?

I'm available on [Day] between [Time] or [Day] between [Time].

Looking forward to connecting.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

---

## Email Mistakes to Avoid

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "Please revert." | "Please reply." |
| "Do the needful." | "Please handle this." |
| "Kindly do this." | "Could you please do this?" |
| "I will do it tomorrow only." | "I will do it tomorrow." |
| Starting with "Dear Sir/Madam" every time | Use their name when you know it |

---

## Email Tone Guide

| Recipient | Tone | Opening |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Manager | Formal-professional | "Hi [Name]," or "Dear [Name]," |
| Colleague (same level) | Semi-formal | "Hi [Name]," |
| Client (first email) | Formal | "Dear [Name]," |
| Client (ongoing) | Professional | "Hi [Name]," |
| Team group email | Casual-professional | "Hi team," or "Hello everyone," |

---

## Speaking Task

Write all 3 emails today. Then **read each one aloud** — this trains both writing AND speaking at once.
