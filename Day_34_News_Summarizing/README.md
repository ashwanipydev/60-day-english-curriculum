## Today's Speaking Challenge

> Summarize a news article you read — speak it out loud

---

## Why Summarizing is a Key Office Skill

In every meeting, someone needs to summarize:
- "So what you're saying is..."
- "To recap, we decided..."
- "The key points were..."

Summarizing in English requires you to understand, process, and re-express ideas — which builds fluency fast.

---

## The 5-Sentence Summary Method

After reading any article, email, or update, summarize it in exactly 5 sentences:

1. **What is this about?** (Topic sentence)
2. **What is the main point?** (Key fact/argument)
3. **What is the evidence or detail?** (Supporting information)
4. **What is the implication?** (So what? Why does it matter?)
5. **What is the conclusion/recommendation?** (Action or outcome)

---

## Reporting Language

| Use | Phrase |
|-----|--------|
| Introducing news | "According to [source]..." |
| Saying what was said | "The article reports that..." |
| Main point | "The key finding is that..." |
| Your opinion | "In my view, this means..." |
| Conclusion | "Overall, it seems that..." |

---

## Practice Summary

Here's a short update to summarize:

> *"The company has announced that all employees will switch to a hybrid work model starting next month. Under the new policy, employees will work from the office on Tuesdays and Thursdays, and from home on the remaining days. The management said this decision was made based on employee feedback and productivity data. The new policy does not apply to customer-facing roles."*

Write/speak your 5-sentence summary of this update.

---

## Speaking Task

Read any one news article (any topic). Then record a **2-minute spoken summary** using the 5-sentence method and the reporting language phrases.
