## The Polished Written Introduction

Write a 150-word professional introduction. This could be used for:
- LinkedIn "About" section
- Conference bio
- Introductory email to a new team/client
- Job application cover letter opening

**Template:**

> "[Name] is a [role] with [X years] of experience in [industry/field]. Currently working at [company], [Name] specializes in [area of expertise]. In this role, [he/she/they] is responsible for [key responsibilities].
>
> [Name]'s key strengths include [2–3 strengths]. A recent highlight was [achievement or project].
>
> [Name] is passionate about [genuine interest related to work]. Outside of work, [optional: brief personal detail].
>
> [Name] is currently focused on [goal or current project] and is always open to [networking/collaboration/new opportunities]."

---

## Refine Your Speaking Introduction

Now take your written introduction and speak it. But don't read it word for word — use it as a guide and speak naturally.

**Practice 5 times:**
1. Read it: understand the content
2. Speak with notes: use bullet points
3. Speak without notes: try from memory
4. Record it: listen back
5. Refine: fix any awkward parts, then record again

---

## Final Check: The 7 Signs of English Confidence

- [ ] You speak without long pauses
- [ ] You use vocabulary naturally (not translating in your head)
- [ ] You ask and answer questions smoothly
- [ ] You can hold a conversation for 5+ minutes
- [ ] You write professional emails without major errors
- [ ] You understand 70%+ of English YouTube videos
- [ ] You think in English for at least some part of your day

---

## Speaking Task

Write your introduction (150 words) → then record the spoken version (90 seconds) without reading it word for word.
