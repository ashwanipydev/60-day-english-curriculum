# Modal Verbs — Complete Guide
### For Hindi-Speaking English Learners

> Modal verbs are helper verbs that come before the main verb. They show possibility, permission, ability, obligation, or politeness.
> Hindi में: ये वो words हैं जो बताते हैं — "सकता हूँ, चाहिए, होगा, होना चाहिए"

**Formula:** `Subject + Modal + Main Verb (base form)`
Example: I **can** speak. / She **should** send the email. / We **must** finish.

---

## The 9 Modals You Need

| Modal | Meaning | Hindi | Example |
|-------|---------|-------|---------|
| **Can** | Ability / casual request | सकता हूँ / क्या आप | Can you help me? |
| **Could** | Polite request / past ability | क्या आप कृपया | Could you send the file? |
| **Will** | Future / promise | करूँगा / होगा | I will complete it today. |
| **Would** | Very polite / conditional | क्या आप... (formal) | Would you like to join? |
| **Should** | Advice / recommendation | चाहिए | You should review this. |
| **Must** | Strong necessity / rule | ज़रूर करना है | We must submit by Friday. |
| **May** | Formal permission / possibility | शायद / क्या मैं | May I share my screen? |
| **Might** | Weak possibility | शायद (कम chance) | I might be late today. |
| **Need to** | Necessity (informal) | ज़रूरत है / करना है | I need to finish this now. |

---

## The Golden Rule

After a modal verb, the main verb is **always in base form** — no 's', no 'ing', no 'ed'.

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---------|---------|
| She can goes. | She can go. |
| You should sending. | You should send. |
| I must finished. | I must finish. |

**Exception:** "need to" always keeps "to" — it's the only one.

## Each Modal in Detail

### Can — Ability / Informal Request
- "I can speak Hindi and English." (Ability)
- "Can you help me with this task?" (Informal request)
- "She can join the call from home." (Possibility)

**Common mistake:** "I can to speak English." → ✅ "I can speak English." (Never use 'to' after a modal verb)

**Office use:** "Can you share the presentation?" / "I can complete this by Friday." / "Can we schedule a call tomorrow?"

---

### Could — Polite Request / Past Ability
- "Could you please send me the report?" (Polite request — more formal than 'can')
- "I could run 10 km when I was younger." (Past ability)
- "Could we move the meeting to 3 PM?" (Formal suggestion)

**Common mistake:** "Could you to check this email?" → ✅ "Could you check this email?"

**Office use:** "Could you please review this document?" / "Could we reschedule to tomorrow?" / "Could you clarify what you mean by this?"

---

### Will — Future Promise / Decision Now
- "I will send you the file today." (Promise — decided right now)
- "The meeting will start at 9 AM." (Scheduled future event)
- "Will you attend the workshop?" (Question about future plan)

**Common mistake:** "I will to finish the task." → ✅ "I will finish the task."

**Office use:** "I will keep you updated." / "Will you be able to present tomorrow?" / "The report will be ready by 5 PM."

---

### Would — Very Polite Request / Conditional
- "Would you like to join us for lunch?" (Very polite invitation)
- "I would appreciate your feedback." (Polite statement)
- "Would it be possible to extend the deadline?" (Formal conditional request)

**Common mistake:** "Would you to review this?" → ✅ "Would you review this?"

**Office use:** "Would you be available for a quick call?" / "I would like to discuss this further." / "Would it be possible to share an update?"

---

### Should — Advice / Recommendation
- "You should proofread the email before sending." (Advice)
- "We should discuss this in the next meeting." (Recommendation)
- "Should I cc the manager on this?" (Asking for advice)

**Common mistake:** "You should to call him." → ✅ "You should call him."

**Office use:** "You should cc the client on this email." / "We should review the budget before approving." / "Should I prepare a summary slide?"

---

### Must — Strong Necessity / Rule
- "You must submit the report by Friday." (Strict deadline)
- "Attendees must register in advance." (Rule/requirement)
- "I must speak to the manager about this." (Strong personal necessity)

**Common mistake:** "You must to attend the training." → ✅ "You must attend the training."

**Office use:** "All team members must attend the all-hands." / "The data must be backed up daily." / "This issue must be resolved before deployment."

---

### May — Formal Permission / Possibility
- "May I share my screen?" (Formal permission — use this in office meetings)
- "The results may vary." (Possibility)
- "May I ask you a question?" (Very polite, formal)

**Common mistake:** "May I to ask something?" → ✅ "May I ask something?"

**Office use:** "May I take a moment to explain?" / "This may affect our timeline." / "May I suggest an alternative approach?"

---

### Might — Weak Possibility (less certain than May)
- "I might be late to the meeting today." (Not sure)
- "We might need to revise the plan." (Possibility, not certain)
- "She might have already sent the email." (Uncertain past action)

**Common mistake:** "I might to join the call." → ✅ "I might join the call."

**Office use:** "This might cause some delays." / "I might need more time to review this." / "The client might request changes."

---

### Need to — Necessity (informal, everyday)
- "I need to finish this before the deadline." (Personal necessity — less strong than 'must')
- "We need to discuss the budget." (Group necessity)
- "Do you need to leave early today?" (Asking about someone's necessity)

**Common mistake:** "I need finish this task." → ✅ "I need to finish this task." ('Need to' always needs 'to' — unlike other modals!)

**Office use:** "I need to reschedule our meeting." / "We need to align on the timeline." / "Do you need to loop in anyone else?"

## Politeness Levels — Same Request, Different Tone

| Level | Sentence |
|-------|----------|
| Rude | "Send me the file." |
| Okay | "Can you send me the file?" |
| Good | "Could you please send me the file?" |
| Best | "Would you be able to send me the file when you get a chance?" |

## 10 Common Mistakes Hindi Speakers Make

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right | Why |
|---------|---------|-----|
| I can to speak English. | I can speak English. | Never use 'to' after modal verbs (except 'need to'). |
| She musts attend the meeting. | She must attend the meeting. | Modal verbs NEVER take 's' for he/she/it. |
| You should to call him. | You should call him. | Base form after modal, no 'to'. |
| Can you please to send the file? | Can you please send the file? | After 'can', base form directly. |
| I am willing to can go. | I can go. / I am willing to go. | Two modals cannot be used together. |
| You must to register before tomorrow. | You must register before tomorrow. | Must + base form, no 'to'. |
| May I to ask a question? | May I ask a question? | May + base form directly. Very common mistake. |
| He might to be late. | He might be late. | Might + base form, no 'to'. |
| I would like going. | I would like to go. | Special case: 'would like' takes 'to' + verb. |
| We could went yesterday. | We could have gone yesterday. | Past with modals: modal + have + past participle. |

---

## Quick Memory Tips

- **More formal →** can → could → would (each step = more polite)
- **Never change form** — no cans, wills, shoulds — modal verbs never take 's' or 'ed'
- **Should vs Must** — Should = gentle advice, Must = strong necessity
- **May vs Might** — May = ~50% chance, Might = ~20-30% chance
