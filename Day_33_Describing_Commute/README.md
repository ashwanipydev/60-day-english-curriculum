## Describing Experiences in English

Being able to describe your daily experience fluently is a core skill. This is where English becomes a living language — not just classroom sentences.

---

## Useful Language for Descriptions

**Setting the scene:**
- "Every morning, I..."
- "It takes me about [time] to..."
- "The journey involves..."
- "I usually..."

**Describing what you see:**
- "Along the way, you can see..."
- "The road is usually..."
- "At peak hours, the traffic..."

**Describing feelings:**
- "It can be quite [tiring/relaxing/busy]."
- "I use the time to..."
- "The best part is..."
- "The most annoying thing is..."

---

## Sample Description

> "My commute takes about 45 minutes. I take the metro from [station] to [station], then walk about 10 minutes to the office. During peak hours, the metro can be very crowded, so I try to leave home before 8:30. I usually listen to English podcasts during the journey, which helps me learn while commuting. The best part is that I have 45 minutes of 'me time' to listen, think, or sometimes just relax."

## Speaking Task

Record a **2-minute voice note** describing your commute. Include: how long it takes, how you travel, what you see, how you feel about it, and what you do during the journey.
