## Beyond "My Name is..."

A strong self-introduction = first impression. By now, you should be beyond the basic version.

---

## The 3 Versions

### Version 1: 30 Seconds (Elevator Pitch)
> "Hi, I'm [Name]. I work as a [role] at [company], focused on [main area]. I've been doing this for [X years]. Nice to meet you."

### Version 2: 2 Minutes (Interview/Networking)
> "Good morning, I'm [Name]. I currently work as a [role] in the [department] team at [company]. I've been in this role for [duration] and primarily work on [2-3 key responsibilities]. Before this, I worked at [previous company] where I handled [what]. Outside work, I'm interested in [1-2 interests]. I'm always looking to [growth goal]. Glad to be here!"

### Version 3: Professional Email Introduction
> "Dear [Name],
>
> I hope this email finds you well. My name is [Name] and I work as a [role] at [company]. I'm reaching out because [reason for contact]. I've been in the [industry/field] for [X years] and specialize in [area].
>
> I look forward to connecting with you.
>
> Best regards,
> [Name]"

---

## What Makes an Introduction Great

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| Just your name | Name + role + what you do |
| Generic words | Specific responsibilities |
| No energy | Confident, warm tone |
| Too long | Under 2 minutes |
| Trailing off | Clear ending |

---

## Speaking Task

Practice all 3 versions of your introduction today. Record each one. Listen back and refine. By Day 30, you should be able to deliver Version 2 smoothly.
