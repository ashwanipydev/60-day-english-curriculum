## Today's Speaking Challenge

> Describe a photo from your phone in English

---

## Why Photo Description Matters

Describing visuals is used in:
- Presentations ("As you can see in this slide...")
- Video calls ("I'm going to share my screen now...")
- Social conversations ("I was at this amazing place...")
- Job interviews ("Can you walk us through this project?")

---

## How to Describe a Photo

**Structure:**

1. **Overall scene:** "This photo shows..."
2. **Foreground:** "In the front, you can see..."
3. **Background:** "In the background, there is/are..."
4. **Details:** "I notice that..."
5. **Context/Story:** "This was taken at/during..."
6. **Feelings:** "Looking at this, I feel..."

---

## Vocabulary for Visual Descriptions

**Location words:**
- in the centre / on the left / on the right
- in the foreground / in the background
- at the top / at the bottom / in the corner

**Describing people:**
- "There appears to be a person..."
- "She/he is wearing..."
- "They seem to be [doing something]."

**Describing objects:**
- "There is a [object] which appears to be..."
- "On the [location], there is/are..."

**Describing atmosphere:**
- "The mood is [peaceful/busy/formal/casual]."
- "The lighting suggests it's [morning/evening/indoors]."

---

## Practice: Describe This Scene

Pick any photo from your phone — it could be:
- A photo of your office or home
- A holiday photo
- A photo from a recent event

Describe it for **2 full minutes** using the structure above.

---

## Speaking Task

Pick a photo from your phone. Describe it in full using today's structure. Record yourself. Try to speak for at least 90 seconds without stopping.
