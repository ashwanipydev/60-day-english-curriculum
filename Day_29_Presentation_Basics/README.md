## The 4-Part Presentation Structure

Every great presentation follows this structure:

```
1. OPEN     — Grab attention + tell them what you'll cover
2. BODY     — Your 3 main points
3. CLOSE    — Summarize + call to action
4. Q&A      — Handle questions confidently
```

---

## Opening Phrases

- "Good morning everyone. Today I'll be talking about..."
- "I'd like to start by asking you a question..."
- "Today, I want to share with you..."
- "By the end of this presentation, you will understand..."

## Transitioning Between Points

- "Moving on to the next point..."
- "Now let's look at..."
- "This brings me to my second point..."
- "As you can see from this slide..."
- "Let me highlight the most important aspect here..."

## Showing Data / Charts

- "This graph shows..."
- "As you can see here..."
- "The numbers tell us that..."
- "What's interesting about this data is..."

## Closing Phrases

- "To summarize the key points..."
- "In conclusion, I'd like to emphasize..."
- "The main takeaway is..."
- "Thank you for your time and attention."
- "I'm happy to take any questions."

---

## Handling Questions

| Situation | What to Say |
|-----------|-------------|
| Need time to think | "That's a great question. Let me think about that..." |
| Don't know | "I don't have that information right now, but I'll find out and get back to you." |
| Question is unclear | "Could you clarify what you mean by [X]?" |
| Strong question | "That's a really important point. I'm glad you raised it." |

---

## Speaking Task

Prepare a 3-minute mini-presentation on ANY topic (your project, your city, a hobby, a book). Use the 4-part structure. Record it and listen back.
