## Two Types of Questions

### Type 1: Yes/No Questions

**Formula:** Helper verb + Subject + Main verb?

Reverse the subject and helper verb from a statement:

| Statement | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| You **are** working. | **Are** you working? |
| She **can** attend. | **Can** she attend? |
| They **will** join. | **Will** they join? |
| He **has** finished. | **Has** he finished? |

### Type 2: WH Questions

**Formula:** WH-word + Helper verb + Subject + Main verb?

| WH Word | Hindi | Example |
|---------|-------|---------|
| **What** | क्या | What do you do? |
| **Where** | कहाँ | Where are you going? |
| **When** | कब | When will she come? |
| **Who** | कौन | Who is handling this? |
| **Why** | क्यों | Why was the meeting cancelled? |
| **How** | कैसे | How can I help? |

## Office Questions to Master

Learn these 10 office questions by heart:

1. "When is the meeting?"
2. "Who is handling this task?"
3. "Can you share the update?"
4. "What is the deadline?"
5. "How can I help?"
6. "Have you reviewed the report?"
7. "What is the status of this?"
8. "Could you please clarify?"
9. "When will this be ready?"
10. "Who should I contact for this?"

---

## Common Question Mistakes

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "What you are doing?" | "What **are** you doing?" |
| "Where she is going?" | "Where **is** she going?" |
| "Why he didn't come?" | "Why **didn't** he come?" |
| "How you did this?" | "How **did** you do this?" |

**The pattern:** Helper verb always comes BEFORE the subject in questions.

## Practice

Make these statements into questions:

1. She is working late. → _________________?
2. They will finish by Friday. → _________________?
3. He attended the meeting. → _________________?
4. You can send the file. → _________________?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. Is she working late?
2. Will they finish by Friday?
3. Did he attend the meeting?
4. Can you send the file?

</details>

---

## Speaking Task

Ask yourself **10 questions** and answer them out loud. Sit in front of a mirror and pretend you're being interviewed.

Suggested topics: your job, your routine, your team, your goals.

---

## Key Takeaway

> In questions, the helper verb jumps BEFORE the subject. Never ask "What you do?" — always "What **do** you do?"
