## The 3 Types of Difficult Questions

### Type 1: You Don't Know the Answer

**Don't say:** "I don't know." *(sounds unprepared)*

**Instead:**
- "That's a great question. I'll need to look into that and get back to you."
- "I don't have that specific data right now, but I'll find out and follow up."
- "I want to give you an accurate answer — let me check and confirm."

---

### Type 2: The Question is Unclear

**Don't say:** "What do you mean?" *(too abrupt)*

**Instead:**
- "Could you help me understand exactly what you're asking?"
- "Are you asking about [X] or [Y]?"
- "Just to make sure I answer the right question — are you referring to...?"

---

### Type 3: The Question Challenges Your Work

**Don't say:** "That's wrong." or "That's not true." *(defensive)*

**Instead:**
- "That's a fair point. Let me explain my thinking..."
- "I understand the concern. What I found was..."
- "You raise a good question. My approach was based on [reason]. I'm open to discussing alternatives."

---

## Buying Time Professionally

- "That's an insightful question. Let me think about that for a moment."
- "I want to make sure I give you a complete answer..."
- "Give me just a second to consider this properly."

---

## Speaking Task

**Roleplay:** You've just presented a project plan. Your manager asks 3 tough questions:
1. "Why didn't you consider Option B?"
2. "What's your evidence for this approach?"
3. "What if the timeline slips by 2 weeks?"

Answer all 3 professionally. Record your response.
