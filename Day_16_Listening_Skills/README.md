## Why Listening is Hard for Hindi Speakers

English is spoken at **natural speed** — words blend together, syllables get swallowed.
"Want to" sounds like "wanna". "Going to" sounds like "gonna". "Did you" sounds like "didja".

---

## The 3-Step Listening Method

### Step 1: Extensive Listening (Understanding)
- Listen to longer content for overall meaning
- Don't stop at every unknown word
- Goal: understand the message, not every word

### Step 2: Intensive Listening (Detail)
- Take a 1-minute clip
- Listen 3–5 times
- Note every word you couldn't catch

### Step 3: Transcription Practice
- Pause and write exactly what you hear
- Check against the subtitle/transcript
- Focus on the gaps

## Connected Speech: What Native Speakers Actually Say

| Written | Spoken |
|---------|--------|
| want to | wanna |
| going to | gonna |
| have to | hafta |
| did you | didja |
| could you | couldja |
| I am | I'm |
| do not | don't |
| would not | wouldn't |

---

## Daily Listening Target

- **Morning:** 10–15 min English video (with subtitles first, then without)
- **Note:** Write 3 new words/phrases you heard
- **Evening:** Watch the same clip again — notice what you missed the first time

---

## Speaking Task

After watching a 5-minute video today, summarize what you heard in **5 sentences** without looking at subtitles. Record yourself.
