## Phrases You Need Every Single Day

Today we move beyond learning phrases to **using them in real context**.

---

## Morning Office Phrases

| Situation | Phrase |
|-----------|--------|
| Arriving at office | "Good morning! How's it going?" |
| Greeting a colleague | "Hey, how are you doing?" |
| Starting a meeting | "Let's get started." / "Shall we begin?" |
| Checking in | "How's the [project] coming along?" |

## During Work Phrases

| Situation | Phrase |
|-----------|--------|
| Asking for status | "Could you share a quick update?" |
| When stuck | "I'm having a bit of trouble with this — do you have a minute?" |
| Sharing progress | "Just to keep you posted — I've completed [X]." |
| Asking for deadline | "When do you need this by?" |
| Accepting a task | "Sure, I'll take care of it." |
| Can't do it now | "I'm tied up at the moment — can I get back to you in an hour?" |

## Email Phrases

| Use | Phrase |
|-----|--------|
| Opening | "I hope you're doing well." |
| Purpose | "I'm writing to follow up on..." |
| Request | "Could you please review the attached?" |
| Urgency | "I wanted to bring this to your attention as it's time-sensitive." |
| Closing | "Please let me know if you have any questions." |
| Sign off | "Best regards," / "Thanks and regards," |

## End of Day Phrases

| Situation | Phrase |
|-----------|--------|
| Leaving | "I'm heading out for the day. Have a good evening!" |
| Wrapping a conversation | "I'll follow up with you tomorrow on this." |
| Assigning next steps | "The next step is [X]. I'll handle it." |

---

## Practice

Pick 10 of today's phrases. Write them on a sticky note and try to use each one at least once today in a real or imagined situation. Speak them out loud.
