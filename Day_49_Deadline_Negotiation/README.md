---

## Negotiation is Not Argument — It's Collaboration

Many Hindi speakers avoid negotiating at work because they think it will seem disrespectful. In professional English-speaking environments, **negotiating is expected and respected**.

---

## Negotiating a Deadline

**Script:**
> "I want to deliver this well, so I need to flag a concern about the timeline. Given [challenge/workload], I'd like to propose [new date] instead. This would allow me to [benefit]. Would that work for you, or is there flexibility we could discuss?"

---

## Negotiating Resources

> "I've been reviewing the project requirements, and I think we may need an additional [resource/person/tool] to meet the deadline effectively. Could we explore that option? It would help us avoid [risk]."

---

## Negotiating Priorities

> "I currently have [X, Y, Z] on my plate. Given our goals, which should I prioritize? I want to make sure I'm spending my time on what matters most."

---

## Key Negotiation Phrases

| Purpose | Phrase |
|---------|--------|
| Stating a challenge | "I need to flag a concern..." |
| Proposing an alternative | "What if we...?" / "Could we consider...?" |
| Asking for flexibility | "Is there any flexibility on...?" |
| Showing good faith | "I'm fully committed to delivering quality — I just want to make sure..." |
| Reaching agreement | "So we've agreed that [X] — does that work for both of us?" |

---

## Speaking Task

**Roleplay:** Your manager just gave you 3 tasks all due on the same day. Negotiate: ask for priority clarity, propose a revised timeline for one task, and confirm the final agreement. Record the full conversation.
