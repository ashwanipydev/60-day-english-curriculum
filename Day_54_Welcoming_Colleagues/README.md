## Welcome Language: Making Others Feel Included

In diverse workplaces, how you welcome new people matters. Good welcome language is warm, clear, and professional.

---

## Welcoming a New Colleague

**Opening:**
- "Welcome to the team! We're really glad to have you here."
- "It's great to have you on board."
- "I'm [Name], and I'll be working closely with you on [area]."

**Orienting them:**
- "Let me give you a quick overview of how things work here."
- "Feel free to ask me anything — no question is too small."
- "Our team has a daily standup at [time] — I'll make sure you're included."
- "Here's who you'll be working with most..."

**Making them comfortable:**
- "It can feel like a lot at first, but you'll settle in quickly."
- "We're a pretty collaborative team, so don't hesitate to reach out."
- "If you're ever unsure about something, I'm always around."

---

## Team Introduction Email

```
Subject: Welcome [Name] to the Team!

Hi team,

Please join me in welcoming [Name] to our team. [Name] is joining 
us as [role] and will be working on [area/project].

[Name] brings experience in [background]. I know you'll all make 
them feel at home!

[Name], looking forward to working together!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Speaking Task

**Roleplay:** It's Monday morning. A new colleague has just joined your team. Welcome them, introduce yourself, give them a brief overview of the team, and make them feel comfortable. Speak for 2 minutes.
