## Feedback is a Two-Way Professional Skill

In English-speaking workplaces, giving and receiving feedback is normal and expected. The key: **be specific, be kind, be clear**.

---

## The Feedback Sandwich

**Structure:** Positive → Development → Positive

1. **Start with what's good:** "Your report was well-structured and easy to follow."
2. **Add the improvement area:** "One area to work on is the executive summary — it could be more concise."
3. **End on encouragement:** "Overall, great work — I think with a few tweaks this will be excellent."

---

## Giving Positive Feedback

- "I wanted to recognize the great work you did on [X]."
- "Your [action] made a real difference to [outcome]."
- "I was impressed by how you handled [situation]."
- "You showed real initiative when you [action]."

## Giving Constructive Feedback

- "I wanted to share some thoughts on [area] — I hope that's helpful."
- "One thing I noticed is [observation]. What do you think?"
- "Have you considered [alternative approach]?"
- "Going forward, it might help to [suggestion]."

## Receiving Feedback Gracefully

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "That's not true." | "Thank you for the feedback. Could you tell me more?" |
| Silence (embarrassed) | "That's helpful to know. I'll work on that." |
| Arguing immediately | "I understand. Let me think about how to approach this differently." |

---

## Speaking Task

**Roleplay:** Your colleague just completed a presentation. It was mostly good but the Q&A section needed improvement. Give them feedback using the sandwich method. Speak for 2 minutes.
